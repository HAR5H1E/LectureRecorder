Abstract:
This paper aims to examine the predictions made using the flat Earth model provided in
class. Specifically, it aims to document the results of observations made to test whether these
predictions are accurate. The observations focus on whether sunlight can be seen illuminating
clouds below the horizon before sunrise and after sunset, as well as whether the Southern Cross
constellation can be observed in the night sky. The results show that sunrise and sunset do occur ,
and that the Southern Cross is not visible from the observation location. These findings indicate
that the flat Earth model does not adequately account for these observed phenomena.
Introduction:
We aim to test the flat Earth model and whether it can reliably explain the predicted
astronomical phenomena. The model makes assumptions about how sunlight and other celestial
objects should behave with respect to an observer on Earth. If the model is correct, these
predictions should line up with actual observations.

These predictions provide us an opportunity for testing the observations we have made
from the model through direct observation. By observing the behavior of sunlight during sunrise
and sunset and trying to find the certain constellations from the night sky, we aim to evaluate
whether the flat Earth model can reflect the observed world. And to try and evaluate and
interpret the results we got from our observations/Experiments.
Methods:
According to the flat Earth model, the Sun moves above a flat surface and never
approaches or passes below the horizon. It indicates that the Sun is visible above the Earth’s
surface at all times and that the other celestial objects are present above the Earth's
surface.(Sherwood, 2026).
This model was used to generate predictions about how sunlight and other celestial
objects should appear to an observer on Earth. The predictions were tested through direct
observation. Sunrise and sunset were observed from a fixed ground-level location, with attention
given to the appearance of sunlight near the horizon. Nighttime observations were also
conducted to examine the visibility of constellations from the same location. The results of these
observations were then compared to the predictions that were made from the flat Earth model.
Results:
Predictions:
According to the flat Earth model, predictions were made regarding the behavior of
sunlight. Since the Sun moves above a flat surface and does not approach or pass below the

Conclusion:
The results show us that the flat Earth model fails to provide an accurate depiction of the
observed world. The model’s predictions regarding the behavior of sunlight during sunrise and

it allows the Sun Rays to be visible from below the Horizon even though the Sun is not directly
